# Bikeshare Fun Facts, News & Innovations

Facts, news, and innovations for each of the **26 bikeshare systems** tracked in this
project. **Every item links directly to its source.** Each city has three parts:

- **Fun Facts** — history, rollout, records, and quirks (5 for US systems, 8 for others).
- **News & Press** — a **timeline** of the biggest stories across the system's life,
  tagged by theme (Special event · Big day · Conflict · Economics · COVID · Culture).
- **Innovations** — the technological, hardware, and policy firsts it's known for.

For systems outside the US, the extra facts and much of the news come from
**native-language** sources, tagged with a language code: `(FR)` French, `(ES)` Spanish,
`(NO)` Norwegian, `(FI)` Finnish, `(KO)` Korean, `(ZH)` Chinese.

Compiled from public web sources, July 2026. Cities are grouped by region; the id in
parentheses matches the keys in `src/app/constants/cities.ts`.

## North America — United States

### Austin — MetroBike (`austin`)

#### Fun Facts

1. Austin's docked bikeshare launched in **December 2013** as **Austin B-cycle**, running on software from B-Cycle, a division of bike-maker Trek. ([CapMetro](https://www.capmetro.org/news/details/2020/10/02/austin-bcycle-is-now-metrobike))
2. It was **rebranded to MetroBike in October 2020** after Capital Metro (the city's transit agency) took over 50% ownership from the City of Austin. ([CapMetro](https://www.capmetro.org/news/details/2020/10/02/austin-bcycle-is-now-metrobike))
3. The system is a fixture of **South by Southwest** — it logged roughly **37,000 trips during SXSW in 2022** alone. ([Austin Monitor](https://austinmonitor.com/stories/2024/02/capmetro-looks-forward-to-new-electric-era-for-citys-bike-share-system/))
4. Adding **e-bikes** to the fleet supercharged usage: monthly trips climbed from around 12,000 during the pandemic to nearly **28,000 by April 2023**. ([APTA](https://www.apta.com/innovations-in-mobility-shared-bikes-and-e-bikes-in-the-capital-metro-transit-portfolio/))
5. A major **system outage in 2022** forced a near-total revamp of the aging fleet, paving the way for the modern electric-assist bikes riders use today. ([Austin Chronicle](https://austinchronicle.com/news/2022-04-01/metrobike-gears-up-for-revamp-after-major-outage))

#### News & Press

- **Dec 2013 · Special event —** Launched as Austin B-cycle, focused on the downtown core. ([CapMetro](https://www.capmetro.org/news/details/2020/10/02/austin-bcycle-is-now-metrobike))
- **2014 · Big day —** Set a US bikeshare record during SXSW: 17,724 checkouts over the 10-day festival (6.4 per bike/day). ([B-cycle](https://www.bcycle.com/news/2014/10/05/austin-b-cycle-sets-u.s.-bikeshare-system-record))
- **2020 · Economics —** The city handed 50% control to Capital Metro; the system rebranded MetroBike and folded into transit. ([CapMetro](https://www.capmetro.org/news/details/2020/10/02/austin-bcycle-is-now-metrobike))
- **Mar 2022 · Special event —** A software glitch forced a full shutdown March 17–19 — the peak of SXSW — as bikes couldn't be checked in. ([Austin Chronicle](https://www.austinchronicle.com/news/2022-04-01/metrobike-gears-up-for-revamp-after-major-outage/))
- **Fall 2022 · Big day —** Usage peaked above 43,000 trips during the ACL Festival. ([Austin Chronicle](https://www.austinchronicle.com/news/2022-04-01/metrobike-gears-up-for-revamp-after-major-outage/))

#### Innovations

- **Policy —** One of the first US systems folded directly into a regional transit agency (Capital Metro), sharing branding, planning, and fares. ([CapMetro](https://www.capmetro.org/news/details/2020/10/02/austin-bcycle-is-now-metrobike))

### Boston — Bluebikes (`boston`)

#### Fun Facts

1. The system launched on **July 28, 2011** as **Hubway**, with 600 bikes and 60 stations — the operating contract was signed by Mayor Thomas Menino on **Earth Day** that April. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bluebikes))
2. Riders took to it instantly: Hubway logged **more than 100,000 rides within its first 10 weeks**. ([Boston.gov](https://www.boston.gov/news/ten-years-public-bike-share-celebrated-week))
3. In **May 2018** it was reborn as **Bluebikes** under a marketing deal with **Blue Cross Blue Shield of Massachusetts** — hence the blue paint. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bluebikes))
4. It's a genuinely **regional** system: over the years Brookline, Cambridge, Somerville, Everett, Newton, Arlington, Chelsea, Watertown, and Salem all joined. ([Bluebikes](https://bluebikes.com/about))
5. By 2024 the network had grown to about **520 stations and over 5,500 bikes** across 10 municipalities. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bluebikes))

#### News & Press

- **Jul 2011 · Special event —** Hubway launched with a City Hall Plaza ride-out. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bluebikes))
- **May 2018 · Economics —** A six-year Blue Cross Blue Shield deal renamed Hubway → Bluebikes. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bluebikes))
- **2019 · Big day —** Set a single-day record of 10,035 trips (June 4) and passed 10 million rides that September. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bluebikes))
- **2023 · Special event —** Municipal owners announced 750 next-gen e-bikes, growing toward 5,000 bikes / 500 stations. ([Boston.gov](https://www.boston.gov/departments/transportation/bostons-bike-share-journey))
- **2026 · Economics —** A new five-year Lyft contract brought fare increases, the system's first charging docks, and a 20%+ e-bike fleet. ([WBUR](https://www.wbur.org/news/2026/04/23/bluebikes-contract-fares-ebikes-chargers-expansion-quincy))

#### Innovations

- **Policy —** Income-eligible reduced-fare passes; its 2026 contract adds the system's first in-station charging docks. ([WBUR](https://www.wbur.org/news/2026/04/23/bluebikes-contract-fares-ebikes-chargers-expansion-quincy))

### Chattanooga — Bike Chattanooga (`chattanooga`)

#### Fun Facts

1. This small Tennessee city of ~170,000 launched its bikeshare on **July 23, 2012** — **beating bigger cities like New York and Chicago to the punch**. ([Streetsblog](https://usa.streetsblog.org/2013/02/11/chattanooga-bike-share-lessons-for-smaller-cities))
2. It started with **30 stations and 300 bikes** covering a compact 2.5-square-mile downtown area. ([Streetsblog](https://usa.streetsblog.org/2013/02/11/chattanooga-bike-share-lessons-for-smaller-cities))
3. The seed money was unusual: a **$100,000 grant from the local Lyndhurst Foundation** helped get the effort off the ground in 2009. ([Streetsblog](https://usa.streetsblog.org/2013/02/11/chattanooga-bike-share-lessons-for-smaller-cities))
4. When the city surveyed residents beforehand, about **75% said they'd be interested in cycling** if they had access to a bike. ([Streetsblog](https://usa.streetsblog.org/2013/02/11/chattanooga-bike-share-lessons-for-smaller-cities))
5. **E-bikes joined the fleet in 2018** and immediately proved their worth, generating up to **4× more usage** than the pedal bikes. ([Chattanooga.gov](https://chattanooga.gov/stay-informed/latest-news/bike-chattanooga-expands-electrification-more-e-bikes-and-new-e-station))

#### News & Press

- **Jul 2012 · Special event —** Launched with 30 stations / 300 bikes, beating far larger cities to bikeshare. ([Streetsblog](https://usa.streetsblog.org/2013/02/11/chattanooga-bike-share-lessons-for-smaller-cities))
- **2018 · Special event —** Added its first e-bikes, which soon drove up to 4× the usage of pedal bikes. ([Bike Chattanooga](https://bikechattanooga.com/blog/ebike-expansion/))
- **2024 · Special event —** Expanded the e-fleet and opened a dedicated e-station. ([Chattanooga.gov](https://chattanooga.gov/stay-informed/latest-news/bike-chattanooga-expands-electrification-more-e-bikes-and-new-e-station))
- *News on this small system is thin — coverage tracks fleet electrification rather than fare fights or conflicts.*

#### Innovations

- **Policy —** An early proof that a mid-size city (~170k) can sustain docked bikeshare. ([Streetsblog](https://usa.streetsblog.org/2013/02/11/chattanooga-bike-share-lessons-for-smaller-cities))

### Chicago — Divvy (`chicago`)

#### Fun Facts

1. Divvy exists because Mayor Richard M. Daley rode Paris's **Vélib'** system in 2007 and decided Chicago needed the same thing. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Divvy))
2. It launched on **June 28, 2013** with 750 bikes at 75 stations — and within four months had already provided **650,000 trips** covering over 1.5 million miles. ([City of Chicago](https://www.chicago.gov/city/en/depts/cdot/provdrs/bike/news/2013/nov/divvy_expansion.html))
3. It rapidly expanded to **300 stations in 21 of Chicago's 77 neighborhoods** within months of launching. ([Transportation History](https://transportationhistory.org/2019/06/28/this-bicycle-sharing-system-is-six-years-old-today/))
4. Divvy became the **largest bikeshare system in North America**, and its **234-square-mile service area** is the biggest of any such system on the continent. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Divvy))
5. As of late 2025 the system spanned **over 1,130 stations** across Chicago and neighboring Evanston. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Divvy))

#### News & Press

- **Jun 2013 · Special event —** Launched with 750 bikes / 75 stations. ([Transportation History](https://transportationhistory.org/2019/06/28/this-bicycle-sharing-system-is-six-years-old-today/))
- **2015 · Economics —** Launched Divvy for Everyone (D4E): $5 memberships and cash payment for low-income residents. ([Streetsblog Chicago](https://chi.streetsblog.org/2015/07/07/city-launches-divvy-for-everyone-bike-share-equity-program))
- **2019 · Economics —** City approved a $50M Lyft deal to expand Divvy to all 50 wards using dockless e-bikes. ([Streetsblog Chicago](https://chi.streetsblog.org/2019/03/12/lyft-may-spend-50m-to-expand-divvy-to-all-wards-using-dockless-option-ebikes))
- **2020 · COVID —** Went hybrid-dockless, adding 3,500 pedal-assist e-bikes. ([CDOT](https://www.chicago.gov/content/dam/city/depts/cdot/Divvy/Lyft_E-Bike_FactSheet.pdf))
- **2023 · Conflict —** E-bike price increases became a sore point for members. ([Chicago YIMBY](https://chicagoyimby.com/2023/05/divvy-announces-expansion-creating-city-wide-network.html))
- **2025 · Big day —** Riders took a record 6.8 million trips on the year. ([Hoodline](https://hoodline.com/2026/03/divvy-blitz-200-new-bike-stations-poised-to-blanket-chicago-this-summer/))

#### Innovations

- **Tech —** First US bikeshare with in-station docks that charge e-bikes, ending battery swaps (2022). ([City of Chicago](https://www.chicago.gov/city/en/depts/cdot/provdrs/future_projects_andconcepts/news/2022/may/divvy-becomes-first-u-s--bikeshare-system-to-incorporate-ebike-c.html))
- **Policy —** Divvy for Everyone (2015): $5 memberships, no credit card required, cash payment. ([Streetsblog Chicago](https://chi.streetsblog.org/2015/07/07/city-launches-divvy-for-everyone-bike-share-equity-program))
- **Bike —** Adaptive cycling sessions with hand cycles, recumbents, and tandems. ([Streetsblog Chicago](https://chi.streetsblog.org/2022/07/08/cdot-and-divvy-will-hold-adaptive-cycling-sessions-to-make-bike-share-more-inclusive))

### Columbus — CoGo Bike Share (`columbus`) — *closed 2025*

#### Fun Facts

1. Born from an initiative by Mayor Michael B. Coleman, **CoGo launched on July 30, 2013** with 30 stations and 300 bikes. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CoGo))
2. In its first year it was voted **Columbus' Best Alternative Transportation (2013)** and won the Ohio Bicycle Federation's **Horace Huffman Award (2014)**. ([CoGo](https://cogobikeshare.com/about))
3. **E-bikes arrived in June 2020**, and the system eventually grew to **80+ stations and 600+ bikes**. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CoGo))
4. **2023 was its record year**, with over **65,000 trips** logged. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CoGo))
5. CoGo shut down on **February 28, 2025**, as Columbus transitioned to a new unified dockless mobility program — making it the one system in this project to be fully retired. ([Columbus Underground](https://columbusunderground.com/cogo-bike-share-ending-service-this-month-we1/))

#### News & Press

- **Jul 2013 · Special event —** Launched with 300 bikes / 30 stations. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CoGo))
- **2018 · Economics —** Acquired by Lyft. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CoGo))
- **Jun 2020 · COVID —** Added e-bikes. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CoGo))
- **2023 · Big day —** Record year with 65,000+ trips. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CoGo))
- **Feb 2025 · Conflict —** Shut down as Columbus switched to a single dockless vendor (Veo). ([Columbus Underground](https://columbusunderground.com/cogo-bike-share-ending-service-this-month-we1/))

#### Innovations

- **Policy —** A first-wave Alta/Motivate small-city system, ultimately folded into a single dockless mobility vendor in 2025. ([Columbus Underground](https://columbusunderground.com/city-to-replace-cogo-bikes-scooters-with-single-vendor-bw1/))

### Jersey City — Citi Bike (`jersey_city`)

#### Fun Facts

1. On **September 21, 2015**, Citi Bike crossed the Hudson to Jersey City — its **first expansion outside New York City**. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citi_Bike))
2. It launched with **35 stations and 350 bikes**, fully compatible with the NYC system so one membership worked on both sides of the river. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citi_Bike))
3. The rollout wasn't universally loved: **some residents objected over lost car parking spots**. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citi_Bike))
4. Demand was strong enough that Jersey City got its **first expansion in July 2016** — another 15 stations and 150 bikes. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citi_Bike))
5. Mayor Steven Fulop framed it as **transit equity**, aiming to connect Heights and Greenville neighborhoods that lacked easy PATH access to the train. ([Jersey City Times](https://jcitytimes.com/mayor-announces-major-citi-bike-expansion/))

#### News & Press

- **Aug 2015 · Conflict —** Launched to some resident objections over lost car parking. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citi_Bike))
- **2020 · Economics —** Jersey City and Hoboken signed a five-year Lyft deal; e-bikes returned. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citi_Bike))
- **May 2021 · Special event —** Hoboken joined (29 stations / 300 bikes), unifying the NJ system. ([Hoboken](https://www.hobokennj.gov/news/mayor-bhalla-mayor-fulop-and-lyft-launch-unified-citi-bike-system-in-hoboken-and-jersey-city))
- **Sep 2021 · Big day —** Citi Bike set a regional single-day record of 135,005 rides (Sept 11). ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citi_Bike))
- **2024 · Special event —** Rolled out a first-in-region overflow-parking pilot as ridership neared 1M/yr. ([Hoboken](https://www.hobokennj.gov/news/hoboken-jersey-city-roll-out-first-in-the-region-overflow-parking-pilot-for-citi-bike-as-annual-ridership-approaches-1-million))

#### Innovations

- **Policy —** Piloted a first-in-region "overflow parking" scheme for full stations (2024). ([Hoboken](https://www.hobokennj.gov/news/hoboken-jersey-city-roll-out-first-in-the-region-overflow-parking-pilot-for-citi-bike-as-annual-ridership-approaches-1-million))

### Los Angeles — Metro Bike Share (`los_angeles`)

#### Fun Facts

1. Metro Bike Share launched on **July 7, 2016** with 1,000 bikes and 65 stations in Downtown LA. ([Metro Source](https://thesource.metro.net/2016/07/07/its-official-metro-bike-share-program-launches-in-dtla/))
2. It was the **first bikeshare system in the U.S. to be integrated into a city's existing transit system** — you can pay with the same regional **TAP card** used on Metro buses and trains. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metro_Bike_Share))
3. LA was a notable **latecomer**: an earlier attempt failed in 2013, leaving it the only one of the ten largest U.S. cities without a bikeshare at the time. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metro_Bike_Share))
4. Uptake was quick — nearly **5,000 rides** were taken in the days right after launch. ([Metro Source](https://thesource.metro.net/2016/07/07/its-official-metro-bike-share-program-launches-in-dtla/))
5. The $11-million system is a **joint project of LA Metro and the City of Los Angeles**, operated by Bicycle Transit Systems. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metro_Bike_Share))

#### News & Press

- **Jul 2016 · Special event —** Launched in DTLA with TAP-card transit integration. ([Metro Source](https://thesource.metro.net/2016/07/07/its-official-metro-bike-share-program-launches-in-dtla/))
- **2017 · Special event —** Expanded to Pasadena and the Port of LA. ([City of LA](https://lacity.gov/news/metro-announces-bike-share-expansions-pasadena-port-la))
- **2018 · Economics —** Cut single-ride price to $1.75 but pulled out of Pasadena (closed that August). ([Streetsblog LA](https://la.streetsblog.org/2018/07/13/metro-bike-share-update-cheaper-prices-bye-pasadena-hello-usc))
- **2020 · Special event —** Temporarily closed Venice / West LA / Santa Monica to swap to smart-dock bikes. ([Streetsblog LA](https://la.streetsblog.org/2020/10/05/metro-converting-westside-bike-share-system-to-smart-dock))

#### Innovations

- **Policy —** First US bikeshare integrated into the city's transit fare system — pay with the same TAP card as buses and trains. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metro_Bike_Share))

### New York City — Citi Bike (`nyc`)

#### Fun Facts

1. Citi Bike launched on **May 27, 2013** with 6,000 bikes at 332 stations — at the time, the **largest bikeshare program in the United States**. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citi_Bike))
2. The rollout was delayed from a planned 2011 opening by software problems and by **Hurricane Sandy**, which flooded a warehouse of bikes. ([Lyft Blog](https://www.lyft.com/blog/posts/flood-sweat-and-gears-the-tumultuous-triumphant-history-of-citi-bike))
3. In its **first 200 days**, riders took **5.8 million trips** covering more than 11 million miles. ([NYC DOT](https://www.nyc.gov/html/dot/html/pr2013/pr13-067.shtml))
4. It keeps smashing records: **193,645 rides on September 20, 2024**, and a monthly record of **over 5.1 million rides in October 2024**. ([NYC Mayor's Office](https://www.nyc.gov/mayors-office/news/2024/12/mayor-adams-major-expansion-citi-bike-service-outer-boroughs-ridership-continues))
5. Its busiest stations cluster around transit megahubs — **Grand Central, Penn Station, and Union Square**. ([Next City](https://nextcity.org/urbanist-news/data-citi-bike-new-york))

#### News & Press

- **May 2013 · Special event —** Launched with 6,000 bikes after a delay caused by Hurricane Sandy. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citi_Bike))
- **Jul 2017 · Big day —** Hit 70,286 trips in a day — then the most of any system in the Western world outside Paris. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citi_Bike))
- **Sep 2019 · Big day —** Passed 90,000 rides in a single day for the first time. ([Streetsblog](https://nyc.streetsblog.org/2019/09/06/citi-bike-hits-new-record-with-more-than-90000-rides-in-a-single-day))
- **Mar 2020 · COVID —** Ridership surged as New Yorkers avoided the subway. ([Streetsblog](https://nyc.streetsblog.org/2020/03/12/boom-new-citi-bike-stats-show-cycling-surge-is-real-but-mayor-is-not-acting))
- **Sep 2022 · Big day —** Smashed the record with 138,694 trips (Sept 14). ([Streetsblog](https://nyc.streetsblog.org/2022/09/09/citi-bike-smashes-its-single-day-ridership-record))
- **Jul 2023 · Big day —** New peak of ~143,200 trips (July 21). ([amNY](https://www.amny.com/nyc-transit/citi-bike-record-breaking-month-ridership-july-2023/))
- **2026 · Economics —** Fifth straight year of price hikes; annual membership reached ~$220 (from $95 in 2013). ([amNY](https://www.amny.com/news/citi-bike-rate-hikes-2026/))

#### Innovations

- **Tech —** Bike Angels, a gamified rebalancing program that rewards riders for moving bikes to empty stations — now ~30–40% of all rebalancing. ([Citi Bike](https://citibikenyc.com/bike-angels))
- **Policy —** Reduced Fare Bikeshare: $5/month memberships for NYCHA residents and SNAP recipients. ([Citi Bike](https://citibikenyc.com/community-programs/reducedfare))

### Philadelphia — Indego (`philadelphia`)

#### Fun Facts

1. Indego launched on **April 23, 2015**, and **equity was baked in from day one** — about **a third of its opening stations were placed in low-income communities**. ([Mobility Lab](https://mobilitylab.org/research/bike/bikeshare-has-an-equity-problem-and-philadelphia-is-tackling-it/))
2. It offers a **$5 Access Pass** for low-income residents (versus the standard $17/month) and a **cash-payment option** for people without credit cards. ([Mobility Lab](https://mobilitylab.org/research/bike/bikeshare-has-an-equity-problem-and-philadelphia-is-tackling-it/))
3. The equity work shows in the numbers: as of 2018, **45% of passholders were nonwhite** and **35% earned under $25,000**. ([Mobility Lab](https://mobilitylab.org/research/bike/bikeshare-has-an-equity-problem-and-philadelphia-is-tackling-it/))
4. Annual ridership topped **1 million for the first time in 2023**, then hit a record **1.3 million in 2024**. ([City of Philadelphia](https://www.phila.gov/2025-04-23-indego-bike-share-program-marks-10-years-over-8-million-rides-with-anniversary-celebration/))
5. Indego celebrated its **10th anniversary in April 2025**, having surpassed **8 million lifetime rides**. ([City of Philadelphia](https://www.phila.gov/2025-04-23-indego-bike-share-program-marks-10-years-over-8-million-rides-with-anniversary-celebration/))

#### News & Press

- **Apr 2015 · Special event —** Launched with equity built in (a third of stations in low-income areas). ([Mobility Lab](https://mobilitylab.org/research/bike/bikeshare-has-an-equity-problem-and-philadelphia-is-tackling-it/))
- **2018 · Conflict —** Ended its single-ride pass after a wave of fraud and bike theft. ([City of Philadelphia](https://www.phila.gov/2024-08-01-city-of-philadelphia-and-bicycle-transit-systems-reintroduce-single-ride-pass-for-indego-bikeshare-program/))
- **May 2019 · Special event —** Rolled out 400 pedal-assist e-bikes. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indego))
- **2021 · COVID —** Record ridership as thefts fell; announced a five-year plan to double in size. ([Billy Penn](https://billypenn.com/2021/04/09/philadelphia-bike-share-indego-record-ridership-expansion-sales-tax-ebike-thefts/))
- **2023 · Big day —** E-bike rides overtook classic for the first time; annual rides topped 1M. ([City of Philadelphia](https://www.phila.gov/2025-04-23-indego-bike-share-program-marks-10-years-over-8-million-rides-with-anniversary-celebration/))
- **Aug 2024 · Economics —** Reintroduced the single-ride pass after six years. ([City of Philadelphia](https://www.phila.gov/2024-08-01-city-of-philadelphia-and-bicycle-transit-systems-reintroduce-single-ride-pass-for-indego-bikeshare-program/))

#### Innovations

- **Policy —** Built around equity from launch: a $5 Access Pass, cash payments, and stations targeted to underserved neighborhoods. ([Mobility Lab](https://mobilitylab.org/research/bike/bikeshare-has-an-equity-problem-and-philadelphia-is-tackling-it/))

### Pittsburgh — POGOH (`pittsburgh`)

#### Fun Facts

1. Pittsburgh's bikeshare launched on **May 31, 2015** as **Healthy Ride**, with 50 stations and 500 bikes. ([NEXTpittsburgh](https://nextpittsburgh.com/latest-news/healthy-ride-bike-share-rebrands-as-pogoh-with-new-stations-and-bikes-half-of-them-electric/))
2. By 2019 Pittsburgh was the **last remaining Nextbike system in the entire U.S.**, as the equipment supplier pivoted to Europe — leaving the aging fleet in need of replacement. ([NEXTpittsburgh](https://nextpittsburgh.com/latest-news/healthy-ride-bike-share-rebrands-as-pogoh-with-new-stations-and-bikes-half-of-them-electric/))
3. In **May 2022** the whole system was replaced and **rebranded as POGOH**, with **half the new fleet being e-assist bikes**. ([NEXTpittsburgh](https://nextpittsburgh.com/latest-news/healthy-ride-bike-share-rebrands-as-pogoh-with-new-stations-and-bikes-half-of-them-electric/))
4. The name is a triple pun: **PGH** sits inside "POGOH," the **O's look like bike wheels**, and the whole thing nods to a **pogo stick** — bouncing joyfully around town. ([POGOH Media Kit](https://pogoh.com/media-kit/))
5. The rebrand launched with 38 stations and 350 bikes, a fresh start after roughly **100,000 registered Healthy Ride users**. ([NEXTpittsburgh](https://nextpittsburgh.com/latest-news/healthy-ride-bike-share-rebrands-as-pogoh-with-new-stations-and-bikes-half-of-them-electric/))

### Washington, D.C. — Capital Bikeshare (`dc`)

### Montreal — BIXI (`montreal`)

#### Fun Facts

1. BIXI launched in **May 2009** and was **North America's first large-scale bikeshare** — the original from which many others descend. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BIXI_Montr%C3%A9al))
2. The name is a **portmanteau of "bicyclette" and "taxi,"** coined by Michel Gourdeau, who won a public naming contest. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BIXI_Montr%C3%A9al))
3. Its **modular, solar-powered docks** — designed to take up the same street space as a parked car — could be installed or removed without altering the pavement. ([Tools of Change](https://toolsofchange.com/en/case-studies/detail/660/))
4. The hardware was so influential that **TIME named BIXI one of the 25 best inventions of the 21st century in 2025**. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BIXI_Montr%C3%A9al))
5. It rolled out big: **5,000 bikes across 300 stations** in its very first year. ([Lyft Urban Solutions](https://lyfturbansolutions.com/blog/2023/09/bixi-a-system-woven-into-montreals-dna))
6. **2024 was a record year: over 13 million trips** — July and September each topped 2 million, and five single days hit 70,000 trips. ([La Presse (FR)](https://www.lapresse.ca/actualites/grand-montreal/2024-11-26/bixi/plus-de-13-millions-de-deplacements-en-2024.php))
7. In **winter 2023–24, BIXI ran through winter for the first time in its history** — about **150 of its 850 stations** stayed open across seven boroughs, averaging ~9,000 trips a day. ([La Presse (FR)](https://www.lapresse.ca/actualites/grand-montreal/2023-10-12/stations-tarifs-velos/bixi-montreal-devoile-son-plan-pour-son-premier-hiver.php))
8. To handle crushing demand, BIXI introduced its first **"megastation"** at the École de technologie supérieure (ÉTS) in Griffintown. ([La Presse (FR)](https://www.lapresse.ca/actualites/grand-montreal/2023-09-28/achalandage-chez-bixi/des-megastations-des-l-an-prochain-a-montreal.php))
